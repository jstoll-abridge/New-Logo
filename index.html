The Headwinds
The market has changed —
our approach must evolve
01
Microsoft + Epic Are In
The ambient AI category is now competitive. Every Abridge 300 prospect is also hearing a pitch from Epic Cheers or Microsoft DAX.
  • →Decision timelines stretching 30–90+ days as buyers run parallel evaluations before committing
  • →Previously uncontested greenfield categories now require head-to-head proof at the CMIO, CNO, and CFO level
  • →Prospects are pausing existing conversations to "see what Epic includes" in upcoming contract renewals
  • →Long sales cycles mean our pipeline velocity has slowed even when Abridge is the preferred solution

Every deal now has a competitive counter-narrative to navigate. Speed and proof at the right level is the response.

02
Incumbents Are Stickier
The "their notes are worse" argument is fading. Displacement requires a fundamentally different playbook than a year ago.
  • →Note quality across all incumbent vendors has meaningfully improved — the gap is narrowing on surface-level demos
  • →Displacement now requires buy-in from CMIO, CNO, CDI, and Finance — not just clinician satisfaction
  • →Multi-year contracts and deep Epic integration complexity raise switching costs and extend evaluation timelines
  • →Competitors are building customer success moats — making it harder to move an account that's "good enough"

Surface-level demos no longer close displacement deals. We need a deeper proof architecture with the right buyer in the room.

03
Messaging Refresh
We built the category on clinician burnout. Every competitor now claims the same. The new economic buyer is the CFO — and we have the proof to win that conversation.
  • →11–14% wRVU uplift — validated at Corewell Health. Converts physician capacity into direct revenue.
  • →14% HCC capture improvement — validated at Riverside Health. Drives risk-adjusted revenue at scale.
  • →$500K–$1M in avoided physician turnover per departure — the retention argument is now a financial argument.
  • →95%+ monthly retention and 30x adoption at Duke in 4 months — proof the product wins where others stall.

Stop leading with documentation. Start leading with revenue. The proof exists — the pitch must evolve to match it.

Commercial Playbook — 2026
Six plays to retool how we sell.
What do we commit to — and in what order?
01
Finance-First Entry
Stop entering through IT. Revenue Cycle and CDI are the new front door.
  • →Lead every executive conversation with a pre-built ROI model tied to their specific payer mix and volume
  • →Position Abridge as a revenue integrity play — the CFO must be in the room at meeting one, not after
  • →A durable revenue cycle offer: real-time risk adjustment gap closure, stronger MA documentation, and reduced prior auth burden at the point of care
02
Service-Line Beachhead
Win a region, site, or facility. Earn the enterprise. Avoid the nonstarter full enterprise sales cycle.
  • →Target the dissatisfied sub-unit — a floor, specialty, or different EMR facility where the incumbent is underperforming
  • →Lock success criteria before the pilot starts — scope, metrics, and exec sponsor agreed at kickoff, not after
  • →One internal win becomes the enterprise proof point and expansion wedge into the broader system
03
Intelligence-Led ABM
Trigger-driven, not spray-and-pray. Hit the right account at the right moment.
  • →Track CMIO/CMO job changes and MSFT contract renewal windows — engage 9–12 months before they re-sign
  • →Persona-sequenced outreach: CMIO gets peer evidence, CFO gets ROI, CNO gets workflow proof — never one message to all
  • →Every trigger, touch, and response logged and owned in Salesforce — intelligence is useless if it's not actioned
04
Dedicated BDR Coverage
Separate prospectors from account management. One BDR per segment.
  • →One BDR per segment — Strategic, Enterprise, National — embedded in territory, not pooled
  • →BDRs 100% dedicated to Abridge 300 prospecting — AEs are support closers only
  • →BDR KPIs: qualified meetings set within the 300 list, pilots kicked off incentivized, anchored by a dedicated Pipeline Day — one day per week reserved exclusively for prospecting and intentional opportunity creation
05
Clinician Champion Network
One peer call opens more doors than ten cold calls. Deploy the installed base.
  • →Activate CMIOs at installed accounts as peer-to-peer openers into white space targets
  • →Map every white space account to a nearby reference site — peer geography is built into the pitch, not an afterthought
  • →Published clinician voices — case studies, conference abstracts — work as always-on demand gen between rep touches
06
Prescriptive Opportunity Pipeline
No opp, no coverage. Pipeline discipline is a team standard, not rep choice.
  • →Every Abridge 300 white space account = mandatory active Salesforce opportunity by end of Q2
  • →New Logo Thursday: weekly cadence — every open opp reviewed, every stuck deal unblocked that same week
  • →Every stage requires a documented next step and committed date — manager-enforced, not optional hygiene
